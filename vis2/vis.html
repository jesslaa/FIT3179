A Glimpse into the World of Billionaires

In today's interconnected global landscape, the world economy is an intricate tapestry of trade, investment, and commerce. Within this vast expanse, a select group stands out: the billionaires. These individuals, through entrepreneurship, inheritance, or investments, have amassed fortunes that rival the GDPs of entire countries. Now it is time to explore into their origins, industries, and influence, diving into their stories within the broader economic context.



How Many Billionaires are there in the World?

Overall Trend

The number of billionaires has been steadily increasing over the years.

Between 1996 and 2014, there was a nearly 4-fold increase in the number - from 423 to 1653. Now let's dive into the data for 2014 specifically.

Wealth Disparity within Billionaires

The top 100 billionaires held approximately 1/3 of the total wealth amongst those 1653 billionaires in 2014, showcasing a concentration of wealth within a small group.

Total Wealth by Region and Age

There's a dense concentration of billionaires between the age range of 40 and 80. The majority of them possess wealth under 20 billion USD, with only a few outliers having substantially higher wealth.

Billionaires from North America and Europe are predominant in the higher wealth brackets. Meanwhile, regions like Sub-Saharan Africa and South Asia have notably fewer billionaires.
